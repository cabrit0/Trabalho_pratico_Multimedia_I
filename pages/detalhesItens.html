    The Innovators

    Ano de Lançamento: 2014

    Autor: Walter Isaacson

    Tipo de Livro: Não ficção

    Outros Livros do Autor: Steve Jobs, Leonardo da Vinci, Benjamin Franklin

    Classificação: 4.2/5

    Resumo Detalhado: "The Innovators" by Walter Isaacson explores the history of technological innovation, highlighting the collaborative efforts that shaped our digital world. From Ada Lovelace to Steve Jobs, Isaacson chronicles the visionaries who revolutionized computing. He emphasizes teamwork, creativity, and resilience as essential components of progress, showcasing the interconnected stories that led to the information age.

    Neuromancer

    Ano de Lançamento: 1984

    Autor: William Gibson

    Tipo de Livro: Ficção Científica

    Outros Livros do Autor: Count Zero, Mona Lisa Overdrive, Pattern Recognition

    Classificação: 4.5/5

    Resumo Detalhado: William Gibson's "Neuromancer" immerses readers in a cyberpunk universe where hackers navigate a virtual realm. Case, a washed-up computer genius, is hired for a heist in cyberspace. Gibson's novel delves into artificial intelligence, virtual reality, and the blurred lines between humanity and technology, laying the groundwork for the cyberpunk genre.

    The Martian

    Ano de Lançamento: 2011

    Autor: Andy Weir

    Tipo de Livro: Ficção Científica

    Outros Livros do Autor: Artemis, Project Hail Mary, The Egg

    Classificação: 4.7/5

    Resumo Detalhado: In "The Martian," Andy Weir crafts a gripping tale of survival on Mars. Stranded astronaut Mark Watney battles against the odds, utilizing his engineering ingenuity to overcome challenges and communicate with Earth. Weir blends humor with scientific accuracy, offering a riveting narrative of resilience and human determination in the face of isolation and adversity.

    Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind

    Ano de Lançamento: 2014

    Autor: Yuval Noah Harari

    Tipo de Livro: Não Ficção

    Outros Livros do Autor: Homo Deus, 21 Lessons for the 21st Century, The Origins of Totalitarianism

    Classificação: 4.8/5

    Resumo Detalhado: Yuval Noah Harari's "Sapiens" provides a sweeping overview of human history, tracing the evolution of Homo sapiens from ancient ancestors to modern societies. Harari explores pivotal revolutions, from the Cognitive to the Scientific, challenging readers to reconsider the impact of shared beliefs and cultural constructs on the human experience.

    The Pragmatic Programmer

    Ano de Lançamento: 1999

    Autor: Dave Thomas e Andy Hunt

    Tipo de Livro: Tecnologia/Programação

    Outros Livros do Autor: Homo Deus, 21 Lessons for the 21st Century, The Origins of Totalitarianism

    Classificação: 4.8/5

    Resumo Detalhado: In "The Pragmatic Programmer," Andrew Hunt and David Thomas offer timeless advice for software developers. Their pragmatic approach emphasizes practical techniques, efficient coding practices, and continuous learning. Packed with actionable tips, the book serves as a guide to crafting clean, maintainable code and fostering a mindset that embraces change and improvement in the ever-evolving world of programming.

    Dune

    Ano de Lançamento: 1965

    Autor: Frank Herbert

    Tipo de Livro: Ficção Científica

    Outros Livros do Autor: Dune Messiah, Children of Dune, God Emperor of Dune

    Classificação: 4.4/5

    Resumo Detalhado: Frank Herbert's "Dune" unfolds on the desert planet of Arrakis, blending political intrigue with ecological themes. The narrative follows Paul Atreides as he navigates a complex power struggle, intertwining mysticism, and environmental awareness. Herbert's masterpiece is a sci-fi epic that delves into the consequences of power and the delicate balance between nature and civilization.

    Thinking, Fast and Slow

    Ano de Lançamento: 2011

    Autor: Daniel Kahneman

    Tipo de Livro: Psicologia

    Outros Livros do Autor: Nudge, Misbehaving, Predictably Irrational

    Classificação: 4.5/5

    Resumo Detalhado: Nobel laureate Daniel Kahneman explores human cognition in "Thinking, Fast and Slow." He distinguishes between the fast, intuitive thinking system and the slower, analytical system, revealing cognitive biases that shape decision-making. Kahneman's work provides valuable insights into the complexities of the human mind and the factors influencing our judgments and choices.

    Ready Player One

    Autor: Ernest Cline

    Tipo de Livro: Ficção Científica

    Outros Livros do Autor: Armada, Ready Player Two

    Classificação: 4.3/5

    The Art of Computer Programming

    Ano de Lançamento: 1968

    Tipo de Livro: Computação

    Outros Livros do Autor: Concrete Mathematics, Surreal Numbers, 3:16 Bible Texts Illuminated

    Classificação: 4.9/5

    Tipo de Livro: Criptografia

    Outros Livros do Autor: Fermat's Enigma, Big Bang

    Classificação: 4.5/5

    Resumo Detalhado: In "The Code Book," Simon Singh takes readers on a historical journey through the art of cryptography.     Hackers: Heroes of the Computer Revolution

    Ano de Lançamento: 1984

    Autor: Steven Levy

    Tipo de Livro: Não Ficção

    Outros Livros do Autor: "Crypto," "In the Plex"

    Classificação: 4.5/5

    Resumo Detalhado: Steven Levy's "Hackers" traces the evolution of the computer revolution through the stories of key figures. From MIT's Tech Model Railroad Club to the Homebrew Computer Club, Levy captures the ethos of curiosity and collaboration that defined the hacker culture. The book celebrates the pioneers who shaped the early days of computing, emphasizing their influence on technology, innovation, and the ethos of hacking as a creative pursuit.
